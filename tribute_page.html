Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel

About the legend

  • Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel was an Indian politician and a senior leader of the Indian National Congress.

  • He played a crucial role in the fight for India's independence and was instrumental in the integration of over 500 princely states into the newly independent India.

  • Born on October 31, 1875, in Nadiad, Gujarat, Vallabhbhai Patel pursued a career in law and became a successful barrister.

  • He was deeply influenced by the teachings of Mahatma Gandhi and joined the non-cooperation movement against British rule in India.

  • Sardar Patel played a pivotal role in organizing various civil disobedience movements and protests.

  • He was imprisoned several times during the struggle for independence but continued to fight for the rights and freedom of the Indian people.

  • After India gained independence, Sardar Patel was appointed as the first Deputy Prime Minister and Minister of Home Affairs.

  • He led the task of unifying more than 500 princely states with the Union of India, using diplomacy and tact to persuade the rulers to join the nation.

  • Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel's leadership, determination, and commitment to unity have left a lasting impact on India.

  • He is fondly remembered as the "Iron Man of India" for his strong will and devotion to the country and its people.

  • Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel is one of the most vital names in India’s struggle for Independence.

  • On 31 st October 2018, his birth anniversary, World’s tallest statue named ‘Statue of Unity’ was established in his memory.

  • The statue with a height of 182 meters (597 ft) stands on the bank of the Sardar Sarovar Dam near Vadodara in Gujarat.

  • Statue Of Unity is a patriotic symbol of the legacy of a man who spent his life in the struggle for freedom.

  • It is a symbol of both the unity of "hearts and the geographical integrity".
